Audio Classification

We provide several test files from AudioSet and SPCv2 to demonstrate how our models behave in these classification tasks.

Speech Command Ver.2

The dataset presents a 35-class single-label speech command recognition task. The decision in test set is made by selecting the command that is predicted to have the highest (top-1) probability. The top-1 accuracy in test set is 97.8% for both LibriTTS and AudioSet pretraining. Eight segments are shared in this demo, whose ground truth labels are listed on top of the audio file. The model correctly predicted the command for all eight files.

AudioSet

AudioSet presents a 527-class multi-label tasks, where various sound sources, events and scenarios are included. Models are finetuned in AudioSet with two settings: AudioSet-2M (summation of unbalanced and balanced subsets) and AudioSet-20k (balance subset alone). The common metric in AudioSet is mean average precision, which does not require a 0/1 binary decision (0-100% probability is used instead). However, for better understanding to the task, we use thresholds optimized on test set itself to produce multi-label decisions.

Label classes are presented on top of the audio file, while predicted tags from AudioSet-2M and AudioSet-20K are presented below the audio file.

Bonus: Music Bandwidth Extension

Bandwidth extension (BWE) is also an important restoration task. As mentioned in the paper, pretraining with AudioSet makes the ViT-AE compatible non-speech tasks. In this bonus session, we offer a demo of music BWE, where the narrowband input at the sampling rate of 11 kHz is converted to 22 kHz. Each track is 30-second long.

In this demo, the ViT-AE pretrained on AudioSet (the same one used in the paper) is fine-tuned with Free Music Archive (FMA) dataset. As the multiplicative ViT-AE or ViT-AE-iSTFT is not suitable to BWE tasks (it is difficult to create new contents by a mask valued between 0% to 100%), we use the proposed additive ViT-AE instead. The additive ViT-AE learns to predict the high frequency components, and sum these components with the narrowband input to extend the bandwidth.

We use log spectral distance (LSD), a common metric for BWE tasks, to evaluate the performance of our model. For the below eight samples, additive ViT-AE significantly improved the average LSD from 4.02 to 0.89 (the closer to 0, the better). We leave in-depth investigation of restoration tasks such as BWE or de-clipping to our future work.
